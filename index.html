Minimum Wage & Key Values (2026)
Statutory Monthly Minimum Wage (SMMLV) — official values effective January 2026

💵 Base SMMLV

$1,750,905 COP

This is the minimum remuneration any worker must receive for a full regular working schedule. No employment contract may set a lower amount.

🚌 Transportation Allowance

$249,095 COP

Mandatory for employees earning up to 2 SMMLV ($3,501,810 COP/month). Not included in the Social Security calculation base (IBC).

ℹ️
The SMMLV is adjusted every year through a governmental decree. All salary thresholds (for integral salary, transport allowance eligibility, parafiscal exemptions, etc.) are expressed as multiples of the SMMLV, so they adjust automatically each year.
02
Ordinary Salary Structure
The most common employment model in Colombia — for employees earning below 13 SMMLV/month
📌

How it works

Under the ordinary structure, the employee receives a monthly base wage plus a transport allowance (if eligible). On top of this, the employer must separately calculate and pay mandatory legal benefits (Prima, Cesantías, Interest on Severance) at specific times of year, plus monthly Social Security contributions. This structure applies to the vast majority of Colombian workers.

03
Mandatory Legal Benefits
Paid separately at specific times — calculated on the base salary (transport allowance may be included in some calculations)
🏅

Prima de Servicios (Service Bonus)

Equivalent to 30 days of salary per year, paid in two equal installments — one by June 30 and the other no later than December 20. Transport allowance is included in the calculation base for this benefit.

June / Dec
💰

Cesantías (Severance Fund)

Equivalent to one month of salary for each year worked. The amount is calculated through December 31 of each year and must be deposited in the employee's designated severance fund administrator by February 14 of the following year.

Feb (deposit)
📈

Interest on Cesantías (Intereses)

An additional payment equal to 12% annually on the total cesantías accrued during the year. Unlike the severance itself, this interest is paid directly to the employee — not into the fund — in January of the following year.

Jan (direct)
🏖️

Vacations (Vacaciones)

15 business days of paid leave per year. This is NOT included in any benefit factor — it must always be paid separately when the employee takes vacation time. It applies under both salary structures.

When taken

💻 PILA System Monthly

All three social security contributions (Health, Pension, ARL) must be paid together through the PILA electronic system every month. The employer withholds the employee's 8% share and pays the full amount.

⚠️ Late payment incurs interest and may result in fines. Employees cannot access health services if contributions are overdue.

⚠️
SENA + ICBF + Health (EPS Employer Share) Exemption — Law 1607/2012 & Decree 114-1:

Certain employers are exempt from three contributions for employees earning less than 10 SMMLV (~$17.5M COP/month), subject to legal conditions:
  • SENA: 2% exemption
  • ICBF: 3% exemption
  • Health EPS — Employer share: 8.5% exemption (the employee's 4% is still withheld and paid)

The 4% Caja de Compensación Familiar always applies and is never exempt. The employee's own SS contributions (Health 4% + Pension 4%) continue to be withheld and paid regardless of these exemptions.

📌 The 30% Benefit Factor covers: Service Bonus (Prima), Severance Pay (Cesantías), and Interest on Severance. Vacations are not included — they must always be paid separately. The integral salary may never be lower than 13 SMMLV; if the SMMLV increases, the salary must be adjusted accordingly.

✅ What it simplifies

  • ✔ No separate Prima payment (June/Dec)
  • ✔ No separate Cesantías deposit (Feb)
  • ✔ No separate interest on Cesantías (Jan)
  • ✔ Single monthly transfer to employee
  • ✔ Lower administrative burden for HR

⚠️ What still applies

  • 📌 Vacations (15 days/year — always separate)
  • 📌 SS contributions on 70% of salary
  • 📌 Overtime and surcharges still apply
  • 📌 Legal indemnities if dismissed
  • 📌 Must be expressly agreed in writing
🔑
Key SS difference: Under integral salary, Health and Pension contributions are calculated on 70% of the agreed integral salary (not 100%), reducing the contribution base but potentially affecting the employee's future pension amount.
